Driving all seven emirates in seven days is ambitious but doable if you treat it as a **loop with one clear highlight per day**, not a deep dive everywhere. The UAE is small on the map — roughly 160 km from Dubai to Fujairah on the east coast — yet each emirate has a distinct angle. This itinerary assumes a rental car, a start/end at Dubai International Airport, and travellers comfortable with highway driving and summer heat planning.

## Before you drive

- **Documents**: Valid driving licence; many nationalities need an International Driving Permit alongside a home licence — confirm before pickup.
- **Salik toll tags**: Rental cars usually include Salik; Dubai gates charge automatically.
- **Timing**: November–March makes outdoor stops pleasant; summer demands dawn starts and afternoon indoor blocks.
- **Base logic**: One hotel move per day max; pack light for trunk space.

## Day 1 — Dubai (arrival & Old Dubai)

Land, collect the car, and stay central or near the creek. Afternoon: **Al Fahidi**, abra across Dubai Creek, Gold and Spice Souks. Evening: Downtown fountain area if energy allows — save Burj Khalifa ascent for an extra day if this is a pure transit start. Night in Dubai.

*If you only have an evening:* prioritize the creek crossing and one sit-down Emirati or Levantine dinner in Al Seef.

## Day 2 — Sharjah & Ajman (culture & coast)

Morning: drive to **Sharjah** (20–40 min). Visit the Museum of Islamic Civilization or Arts Area; respect Sharjah's conservative public norms (modest dress, no alcohol in public spaces). Lunch in Sharjah; afternoon stroll **Ajman** corniche and the old fort — compact, low-traffic. Evening return west or stay in Sharjah/Ajman for cheaper hotels. Night near Dubai/Sharjah border.

*Highlight:* Sharjah's heritage density per kilometre is unmatched in the UAE.

## Day 3 — Umm Al Quwain & Ras Al Khaimah (quiet coast & mountains)

Drive north through **Umm Al Quwain** — stop at the mangroves or Dreamland Aqua Park only if travelling with kids; otherwise continue to **RAK**. Afternoon: **Jebel Jais** viewpoint or timed adventure activity; book zipline slots ahead. Sunset from altitude; descend before road fog risk in winter. Night in RAK beach or mountain hotel.

*Skip if rushed:* UAQ can be a drive-through; RAK deserves the full afternoon.

## Day 4 — Fujairah (east coast)

East on **E89** through the Hajar passes — scenic, occasional heavy-truck traffic. Morning: **Fujairah Fort** and Friday Market area. Afternoon: east-coast beach (Dibba or Al Aqah) or a dive/snorkel boat if certified. Fujairah faces the Gulf of Oman — different water colour and vibe from the Arabian Gulf west coast. Night on the east coast.

*Note:* No direct coast road between RAK and Fujairah; you cross exclaves and mountain roads — allow three to four hours with stops.

## Day 5 — Abu Dhabi (capital culture)

Long transit day: Fujairah to **Abu Dhabi** via E10/E11 (roughly three to four hours with breaks). Afternoon: **Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque** — go late afternoon for cooler light and pre-close timing; dress code enforced. Evening Corniche walk. Night in Abu Dhabi.

*Alternative if tired:* split mosque to Day 6 morning.

## Day 6 — Abu Dhabi (museums or Yas) & Dubai return

Morning: **Louvre Abu Dhabi** or **Qasr Al Watan** (pick one, not both, on a road-trip pace). Afternoon: Yas Island hit (Ferrari World) **or** drive back toward Dubai. Evening: JBR or Marina stroll. Night Dubai.

## Day 7 — Dubai (departure buffer)

Keep flexible for flight time: last-minute **Dubai Mall**/Burj Khalifa if missed Day 1, or desert sunrise safari if flight is evening. Return car with full tank per contract; airport is 15–25 min from most Dubai hubs off-peak.

## Distance and realism check

| Leg | Approx. drive |
| --- | --- |
| Dubai → Sharjah | 30–50 min |
| Sharjah → RAK | 60–90 min |
| RAK → Fujairah | 3–4 hrs |
| Fujairah → Abu Dhabi | 3–4 hrs |
| Abu Dhabi → Dubai | 45–75 min |

Seven days means **some emirates get hours, not days** — especially Ajman and UAQ. That matches the "complete loop" goal: you have stood in each, with memory anchors in Sharjah, RAK mountains, Fujairah sea, and Abu Dhabi mosque.

## Where to add an eighth day

Extra time pays off in **RAK** (second mountain or wadi day) or **Dubai** (desert overnight). Skipping Fujairah shortens the loop but drops the only Gulf of Oman coastline from the circuit — a meaningful loss.

## Practical tips

- **Friday** traffic patterns differ; museums may open later after morning prayers.
- **Fuel** is inexpensive; stations are frequent on highways.
- **Parking**: Paid zones in Dubai/Abu Dhabi city centres; mosques and malls often have free lots.
- **Alcohol**: Only in licensed hotel venues; Sharjah is dry in public.

## Bottom line

A seven-emirate week is a **highlights reel**, not exhaustive exploration. Follow the loop: Dubai → Sharjah/Ajman → RAK → Fujairah → Abu Dhabi → Dubai. Book Jebel Jais and mosque visits ahead, drive east coast daylight, and accept that Ajman and UAQ are brief stops unless you extend. Done properly, you finish with coast, desert, mountain, and capital culture in one passport stamp — and a clear list of where to return when you have another week.
